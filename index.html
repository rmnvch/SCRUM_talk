Historical overview

In rugby football, a "scrum" is used to restart play, as the forwards of each team interlock with their heads down and attempt to gain possession of the ball.

1986

Hirotaka Takeuchi and Ikujiro Nonaka. Harvard Business Review article, 'The New New Product Development Game'. Term 'scrum' was introduced.

Early 1990s

Ken Schwaber used - Advanced Development Methods; Jeff Sutherland, John Scumniotales and Jeff McKenna developed a similar approach at Easel Corporation, referring to it using the single word Scrum

1995 - 2002

Sutherland and Schwaber cooperation, testing and improvements. 1. 1995 paper (Business object design and implementation: OOPSLA '95 workshop proceedings), 2. contributions to the Manifesto for Agile Software Development in 2001, and the worldwide spread and use of scrum since 2002

2009 - NOW

Since 2009, a public document called The Scrum Guide has been published and updated by Schwaber and Sutherland. It has been revised 6 times, with the current version being November 2020

SCRUM basic concepts

SCRUM TEAM

Is a fundamental unit of SCRUM

SCRUM TEAM

  • Scrum Teams are cross-functional
  • The Scrum Team is responsible for all product-related activities
  • The entire Scrum Team is accountable for creating a valuable, useful Increment every Sprint
  • There are 3 roles within SCRUM team - Product Owner, Developers, SCRUM master

Developers

Are the people in the Scrum Team that are committed to creating any aspect of a usable Increment each Sprint.
  • Requested broad specific skills will vary with the domain of work.
  • Self-organizing / self-managing, without externally assigned roles
  • No appointed lead. On a healthy team, leadership emerges and shifts naturally

Product Owner

Declares vision and priorities.
  • Only one per product, even with multiple teams.
  • The Product Owner is one person, not a committee.
  • Decides whether to release the product or to continue developing the product
  • Constantly re-prioritizes the Product Backlog, adjusting any long-term expectations such as release plans.

Scrum Master

Establishes Scrum as defined in the Scrum Guide.
  • Shields the team from external interference and distractions to keep it in group flow (a.k.a. the zone).
  • Serves the team, the Product Owner, and the organization
  • Has no management authority over the team.

SCRUM events

SCRUM events

  • Each event in Scrum is a formal opportunity to inspect and adapt Scrum artifacts.
  • These events are specifically designed to enable the transparency required.
  • Scrum events are divided into 5 stages - 'Sprint', 'Sprint Planning', 'Daily Dcrum', 'Sprint Review', 'Sprint Retrospective'
  • The Sprint is a container for all other events.

Sprint

iS the heartbeat of Scrum, where ideas are turned into value.
  • One to four weeks long. Same length
  • A new Sprint starts immediately after the conclusion of the previous Sprint.

Sprint Planning

  • Which Product Backlog Items are to try to convert to working product during the Sprint.
  • The Product Owner declares the most important to the business.
  • Devolopers are responsible for selecting the amount of work they feel they can implement without accruing technical debt
  • The Product Backlog -> the Sprint Backlog transition
  • Is timeboxed to a maximum of eight hours for a one-month Sprint. (even shorter for shorter Sprints).

Sprint daily

  • The Daily Scrum is a 15-minute event for the Developers of the Scrum Team.
  • It's a posibility for team to inspect their Sprint in progress and create a shared plan for the day.
  • Standing up at the Daily Scrum helps keep it short.

Sprint review

  • Inspecting the outcome.
  • Presenting the results.
  • Reviewing what was accomplished and what has changed.
  • timeboxed to a maximum of four hours for a one-month Sprint. For shorter Sprints, the event is usually shorter.

Sprint Retrospective

  • Scrum team reflect on their own way of working together.
  • The Scrum Team inspects how the last Sprint went with regards to individuals, interactions, processes, tools, and their Definition of Done.
  • The Sprint Retrospective concludes the Sprint. It is timeboxed to a maximum of three hours for a one-month Sprint. For shorter Sprints, the event is usually shorter.

Product Backlog

  • Force-ranked (prioritized) ordered list of what is needed to improve the product
  • It is the single source of work undertaken by the Scrum Team.
  • Visible to all stakeholders
  • Anyone can suggest items
  • Items at top should be smaller (e.g., smaller than 1/4 of a Sprint) than items at bottom

Sptint Backlog

  • The Sprint Backlog is composition of three: the Sprint Goal (why), the set of Product Backlog items selected for the Sprint (what), an actionable plan for delivering the Increment (how)
  • The Sprint Backlog is a plan by and for the Developers
  • Real-time picture of the work that the Developers plan to accomplish during the Sprint in order to achieve the Sprint Goal
  • is updated throughout the Sprint as more is learned

Icrement

Potentially Shippable Product
  • The product capabilities completed during the Sprints.
  • Brought to a usable, shippable state at least by the end of each Sprint, or more frequently than that.
  • Should correspond to Definition of Done (e.g. Be properly tested, Be fully integrated and so on)
  • The Sprint Review !== gate to releasing value.
